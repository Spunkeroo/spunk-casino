What Is Provably Fair Gambling? How to Verify Every Bet You Place

March 6, 2026 · 8 min read · Spunk.Casino

In traditional online casinos, you have to trust that their random number generator (RNG) is actually random and that they aren't adjusting odds against you. You can't verify this — you take their word for it (and hope regulators are watching). Provably fair gambling eliminates this trust requirement entirely using cryptography.

The Core Problem Provably Fair Solves

How do you know a casino didn't see your bet and then decide the outcome? With traditional online casinos, you don't. They generate the result server-side, after you've committed. There's no way to prove it wasn't changed.

Provably fair solves this by having both the casino AND the player contribute to the randomness of each outcome — before the bet is placed. Neither party can change their contribution after the fact without the other detecting it.

How Provably Fair Works: Step by Step

1. The Casino Commits to a Server Seed

Before each bet, the casino generates a random "server seed" and sends you its cryptographic hash (a one-way fingerprint). You can see the hash but cannot reverse-engineer the seed from it. This means the casino is locked into that seed before you bet.

2. You Provide a Client Seed

You also generate a random "client seed" (most casinos let you set this yourself). This seed is combined with the server seed to produce the final outcome. Since you control your seed, the casino cannot know the exact outcome until you've both contributed.

3. Bet Is Placed and Outcome Generated

The game combines: server seed + client seed + nonce (bet number) using a cryptographic function (usually HMAC-SHA256) to generate the game outcome. This is a deterministic mathematical function — given the same inputs, it always produces the same output.

4. After the Bet: Verification

After the bet resolves, the casino reveals the original server seed (not just the hash). You can now independently verify: hash(server seed) = the hash you were shown before the bet. If it matches, the casino didn't change the seed. You can then re-run the HMAC function yourself with the revealed seed and your client seed to confirm the outcome was correct.

The key insight: The casino cannot change the server seed after you've bet (you'd detect the hash mismatch). And they didn't know your client seed in advance. So neither party could manipulate the outcome.

How to Actually Verify a Bet

  1. After a bet, find the "Verify" button in the game history
  2. Note the: server seed (revealed), its hash, your client seed, and nonce
  3. Go to a provably fair verification tool (most casinos provide one, or use any HMAC-SHA256 calculator)
  4. Input the three values and run the function
  5. Compare the output to the game result — they should match exactly

Games That Use Provably Fair

  • Dice: The most common provably fair game. Result is a number 0-100.
  • Crash: The crash point is determined by the combined seed before the round starts.
  • Plinko / Mines / Wheel: Newer games increasingly implementing provably fair systems.
  • Traditional Slots: Most slots are NOT provably fair — they use certified RNGs instead.

Why This Matters for Crypto Gambling

Provably fair is one of the most important innovations in online gambling. It shifts the trust model from "trust the casino" to "verify the math." Combined with blockchain-based payments (no chargebacks, no frozen accounts), it creates a gambling environment that is genuinely fairer than regulated traditional casinos.
